Hippo DAI Lab: Hyper-heuristics for Interpretable Public Policy Analysis

Dr. Jazmin Zatarain Salazar and I direct the Hippo lab. The Hippo lab is a Delft Artificial Intelligence (DAI) lab run by experts in the fundamentals of AI technology along with experts in AI challenges, involving researchers from the Interactive Intelligence section in the Intelligent Systems (INSY) department at the Faculty of Electrical Engineering, Mathematics and Computer Science (EEMCS) and the Policy Analysis section in the Multi-Actor systems (MAS) department at the Faculty of Technology Policy and Management (TPM).

AI for fair, efficient and interpretable public policy

The core goal of the Hippo lab is to advance multi-purpose decision support using collaborative AI and heuristic methods. The lab seeks to address the following major scientific challenges:

  1. How can we integrate subjective notions of ethics and fairness into algorithmic design?
  2. How can we create definitions of succes to find effective and equitable trade-offs in policy search?
  3. How can we develop interpretable AI methods to gain public confidence and provide unbiased support in real decision-making contexts?
  4. How can we support public policy deliberation via collaborative AI methods?
